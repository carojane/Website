Enivrez-vous

Il faut être toujours ivre, tout est là ; c'est l'unique question. Pour ne pas sentir l'horrible fardeau du temps qui brise vos épaules et vous penche vers la terre, il faut vous enivrer sans trêve.

Mais de quoi? De vin, de poésie, ou de vertu à votre guise, mais enivrez-vous!

Et si quelquefois, sur les marches d'un palais, sur l'herbe verte d'un fossé, vous vous réveillez, l'ivresse déjà diminuée ou disparue, demandez au vent, à la vague, à l'étoile, à l'oiseau, à l'horloge; à tout ce qui fuit, à tout ce qui gémit, à tout ce qui roule, à tout ce qui chante, à tout ce qui parle, demandez quelle heure il est. Et le vent, la vague, l'étoile, l'oiseau, l'horloge, vous répondront, il est l'heure de s'enivrer ; pour ne pas être les esclaves martyrisés du temps, enivrez-vous, enivrez-vous sans cesse de vin, de poésie, de vertu, à votre guise.

Charles Baudelaire

This is my favorite poem. In it, Baudelaire tells us that the most important thing in life is to always be drunk. I can raise a glass to that sentiment! Get drunk on life - don't be "a martyred slave to time." Live life to its fullest, and with passion. This is an important lesson, one I sometimes forget, which is why I keep this poem close to remind me.

I adopted Monte while living in Albuquerque from the rescue New Mexico Animal Friends. He's been my best friend for five years, and was already five years old when he came to live with me. I can only imagine the life he lived in those first five years - he has BB pellets lodged in his back and scars from a dog fight - but he is still the sweetest, friendliest dog. Each night, he cuddles with me and we bring each other great comfort.

Monte enjoys life a lot. He is a people watcher and loves going to outdoor terraces at restaurants where he can observe the other diners, paying special attention to see if they drop any food on the ground. When someone is cooking in the kitchen, Monte can always be found keeping an eye on the progress. As a chihuahua, he is blessed with a Napoleon complex that makes him think he is 20x as large as he is when confronting strangers or large dogs.

I highly encourage people to adopt their pets from rescues and from animal shelters, rather than buying purebred dogs or buyin puppy-mill dogs from pet stores. Too many great animals are euthinized for no reason more than to make room in the shelters. Puppy mills are unregulated and often inhumane breeding factories, with profit as the primary motivation, not the health or interest of the dogs. The responsible thing to do is to spay and neuter all pets to avoid the explosion of the stray and feral dog and cat populations. Monte is the most recent of adopted pets to become a part of my family.
